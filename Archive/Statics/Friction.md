[[Reaction Force|Reaction Force]] caused by normal force and material properties

$$F_f = \mu F_N = \mu mg$$
*Friction force = friction coefficient ($\mu$) $\times$ normal force ($F_N$)*
- two types of friction, static (not moving) & dynamic (moving)
	- static < dynamic for most purposes (until heat but that's a separate topic)
- friction is a [[Reaction Force|reaction]] so it's only as strong as opposing force

### Tipping
- friction force must be greater than force applied (so object can catch)
- [[Torque|Moment]] about leading edge from force applied must be greater than moment of gravity
	- this is what causes the tip, as otherwise object would not move ($\sum M = 0$)

### Belt Friction
$$T_2 = T_1 e^{\mu_s \beta}$$
Greater tension = lesser tension multiplied by $e$ to friction coefficient ($\mu$) $\times$  wrap angle (rad.) ($\beta$)
