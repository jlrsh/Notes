- trusses are structures composed of straight members with joints
- members are two-force members, meaning only loaded axially (no bending/shear)
- assumptions for trusses include:
	- joints are free to rotate with no [[Reaction Force|reaction force]]
	- members are two force members
	- loads are only applied at joints
	- weight of members is negligible
	- **compression is a negative force and tension is positive**

### Zero Force Members
- members can be zero load in a truss
- occurs when:
	- two members meet at a joint with no external load
		- no load in a specific direction, can re-orient coordinate plane for equilibrium
	- three members meet at a joint and two are collinear with no external load
		- only one member in $y$ direction, therefore $F_x = F_y = 0$ because $\sum F_y = 0$

### Method of Joints
- one approach to solving truss problems is to calculate loads at every joint
- find reaction force of whole truss first using supports
- then draw a FBD for every joint and write equilibrium equations ($\sum F = \sum M = 0$)
- all unknown forces should be in tension unless found to be otherwise

### Method of Sections
- find reactions similarly to method of joints
- cut truss up into sections and then use forces on each cut "end" to reach equilibrium
