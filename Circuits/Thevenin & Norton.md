### Thevenin's Voltage
Thevenin's Voltage is the open-circuit voltage at load terminals or the difference in voltage between two different terminals

To find $V_{th}$ just remove $R_{load}$ and find voltage difference or transform everything into a singular [[Sources|independent voltage source]] with a resistor in series (Voltage is $V_{th}$)

### Norton's Current
Norton's Current is the current across the load terminals when short-circuited

To find $I_N$ just remove the load resistor and short terminals or source transform into a singular [[Sources|independent current source]] with a resistor in parallel (Current is $I_N$)

### Power for Load Resistor
Some questions may ask for the power across the load resistor
$$P_L = V_L * I_L = \frac{V_I^2}{R_L}=I^2_L*R_L$$
