geometric property of a cross-section quantifying resistance to bending

$$I_x = \int_A y^2 dA$$
*$I_x$ = second moment around $x$, $A$ = area*

![[Polar Moment of Inertia]]
$$J_z = I_x + I_y = \int_A y^2 dA + \int_A x^2 dA$$

### Parallel Axis Theorem
Theorem describing how to find second moment from non-centroid location

$$I = I_{centroid} + Ad^2$$
*$I$ = second moment from non-centroid, $I_{centroid}$ = second moment from centroid, $A$ = area, $d$ = distance from non-centroid to centroid*
