Pressure due to load from a fluid (usually water) distributed over a surface by depth

$$F_p= \frac{1}{2} \rho gz^2 w$$
*$\rho$ is density, $z$ is depth under surface, $w$ is width of container/volume*
- Pressure is a function of depth ($P(z)= \rho gz$)
- Force is a function of pressure so $F = \int_{z_1}^{z_2} P(z) dA = \frac{1}{2} \rho gz^2 w$ where $w$ is width
- Pressure isn't a function of any other dimension so $x$ or $y$ are not included
- Since hydrostatic pressure is a distributed load, it acts at the centroid
	- Centroid for a right triangle is 1/3 of height & width
